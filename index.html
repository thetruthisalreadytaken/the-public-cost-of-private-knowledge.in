The Public Cost of Private Knowledge

For some time now ideas or 'intellectual property' have been seen as property or a private good:

But this isn't reality. Intellectual property is actually a public good:

Using a public good doesn't make it any less available to another. You can't (easily or without modification) exclude it's use from someone else.

Some time ago we decided to convert IP into a private good by creating laws to make it a crime to share intellectual property without permission

We did this in the hope that it would encourage more IP to be created. However what is easily overlooked is in doing this we make a public good artificially scarce and ...

We condemn the vast majority to go without so only a small minority will have an incentive to purchase Intellectual Property.

Ideas could be like a flame jumping from one candle to another until the world is alight.

Instead our artifical marketplace only allows for a scattering of light in a sea of darkness

This might seem acceptable when picturing Intellectual Property as the latest Transformers movie. But picture this:

Each point in the darkness is a child who goes without medicine that could be easily be made available for only it's marginal manufactoring manufacturing cost.

Each point in the darkness is a child who goes without educational material that they could have for only it's marginal distribution cost.
The best minds of our generation could strive to benefit the world instead of the awkward businesses who to try to participate in the artifical marketplace.
Businesses which compete to efficently and brutally advertise and sell what could otherwise be free. Both in cost and in speech. Usually wielding the same technologies which could be used to free it.

  • Think of a world where we innovate and create for all the world instead of just the small minority that makes up the artificial 'IP' market.
  • Algorithms that provide the best schooling for a child instead of the best targeted marketing

  • Where everyone can get any medicine they need for only the small cost of manufactor and distribution.
  • Where everyone can immedately access any book, song, film, or piece of art..
  • A global and public digital library which could be our generation's greatest accomplishment. A digital alexendria that could dwarf any library which has came before.

    • This isn't in support of one piece of legislation or another. This isn't about SOPA or PIPA. These are red herrings- offering an artificial answer to an artificial problem. A detail to be contested so no one will think to challenge the premise. We're at a turning point when it comes to how we will percieve, pursue, and pay for intellectual work as a society .

    Either we will choose to continue to see IP as a loaf of bread - to be bought or sold - or we will see it as a light to be shared. Current patent and copyright law, like any artificial creation, is imperfect. The abstraction of 'intellectual property' is leaking- seen everyday as information technology makes IP shimmer from bread back to light and as we continually commit ourselves through tireless effort to see as it's artifical legal fiction.

    To vigilantly police ourselves and those we trade with to regard ideas as property and to see those who don't as criminals. Because if we don't - if we allow the facade to fall away and allow everyone access to anything - much of what we consider valuable will be effortlessly created and consumed. We will be able to splash in the sum of our human knowedge like a child on the shore and we will changed.
